Apr 2024
Arduino MATLAB C++ Fusion 360

Pick and Place Robotic Arm

Driven by the need for cost-effective, adaptable automation in precision manufacturing and assembly lines, I designed and built a reliable robotic arm capable of executing precise motion control profiles. I created lightweight, 3D-printed components in Fusion 360 and assembled a 3-DOF arm using NEMA 17 steppers and an Arduino Mega. By formulating the forward and inverse kinematics using MATLAB-UART communication to govern the arm's trajectory, the final prototype successfully increased the payload-to-weight ratio by 15% and reduced mechanical trajectory error by 20%, proving its viability for scalable automation tasks.

Smart Energy Meter

In response to aging energy infrastructures lacking real-time node-level consumption monitoring, I created an accessible, non-intrusive IoT monitor for real-time localized power tracking. I engineered a sensor module integrating ZMPT101B and ZMCT103C transducers with an ESP32 microcontroller, and built a locally hosted HTML web dashboard for direct data visualization over Wi-Fi. Ultimately, the system provided ±3% measurement accuracy without requiring circuit rewiring, perfectly demonstrating a scalable multi-node architecture for microgrid applications.

Reflection & Learning

Bridging Disciplines

Through the Pick and Place Robotic Arm project, I realized the critical importance of a multi-disciplinary approach. Applying inverse kinematics mathematically was deeply satisfying, but translating those mathematical models into C++ code on an Arduino Mega and accounting for real-world mechanical tolerance via 3D printed parts from Fusion 360 presented unique challenges. The ~20% reduction in trajectory error taught me that software tuning must be paired with precise hardware fabrication to achieve high fidelity.

The Value of Scalability and IoT

In developing the Smart Energy Meter, I expanded my understanding of IoT architectures and web interfaces. Building an HTML dashboard directly hosted on an ESP32 enhanced system accessibility and demonstrated how raw sensor data (achieving ±3% measuring accuracy) becomes actionable user information. I gained a deep appreciation for non-intrusive, scalable designs that are easy for clients to install safely.

Designing for Human Impact

The Dynamic Audio Monitoring and Narration system shaped my perspective on user-centric engineering. By integrating complex components like multi-threaded Python architectures, WebSockets, and Google Gemini AI, the primary goal was seamless human interaction. Optimizing text wrapping for an OLED display to improve readability by 40% and creating a bash automation script reinforced the principle that sophisticated technology is most effective when it is highly accessible and deployed seamlessly for end-users.
